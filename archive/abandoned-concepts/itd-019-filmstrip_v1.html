Muybridge froze a galloping horse in 1878 with 12 plate cameras tripping in sequence. A modern high-speed camera does the same thing on one sensor, at frame rates he could not have imagined. Every frame is a discrete slice of time. At 60 fps your phone catches one or two slices of this balloon, and misses the pop entirely. At 5,000 fps you see the rupture peel across the rubber. At 500,000 fps the sheet fills with 15,000 frames and every fragment trajectory is captured. The camera is a contact sheet generator. Frame rate is how densely it prints.
