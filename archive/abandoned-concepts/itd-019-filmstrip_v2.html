Muybridge froze a galloping horse in 1878 with 12 plate cameras tripping in sequence. A modern high-speed camera does the same thing on one sensor. Every frame is a discrete slice of time. At 60 fps your phone catches zero frames of a 2 ms spark, and misses it entirely. At 1,000 fps you catch 2 frames, enough to know the spark happened. At 20,000 fps you catch 40, and you can watch the step leader descend branch by branch. The camera is a contact sheet generator. Frame rate is how densely it prints.
