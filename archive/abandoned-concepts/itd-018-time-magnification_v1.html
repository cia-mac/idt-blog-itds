A magnifying glass bends light to stretch space. A high-speed camera captures dense time samples and replays them slowly to stretch time. Same principle, different dimension. Capture rate divided by 30 fps playback gives you the magnification factor. At 5,000 fps you are watching every event 166× larger than it happened. At 500,000 fps, 16,666×. The physical event is always the same 30 milliseconds. What changes is how much of it you can perceive.
