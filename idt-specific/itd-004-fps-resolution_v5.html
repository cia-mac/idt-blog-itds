Select a camera above to explore its resolution and frame rate modes.

The physics: A CMOS sensor reads pixels row by row. Fewer rows means faster readout, which means higher frame rates. Each mode crops the sensor to a specific active area. The window above shows this crop to scale. Smaller window, faster capture. The pixels within the active area are read at full fidelity. This is not downsampling. It is a physical reduction of the readout region.
