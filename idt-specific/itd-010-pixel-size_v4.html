A pixel is a bucket for photons. A 2.5 µm pixel has an area of 6.25 µm². An 11.6 µm pixel has an area of 134.6 µm², collecting over 21 times more light from the same scene. This is why larger pitch sensors can reach ISO 30,000 while smaller pitch sensors sit closer to ISO 3,000. More photons per pixel means stronger signal relative to the sensor's read noise floor, which translates directly to cleaner images at the same exposure settings. The tradeoff is resolution. Fitting fewer, larger pixels onto the same sensor area means fewer total pixels.
