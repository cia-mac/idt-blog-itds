High speed cameras connect through three protocols. Fiber (QSFP) runs long distances at full bandwidth, used by Phoenix, Galileo, Helios, and XS II. USB-C connects compact cameras (XSM, OS II, SugarCube) directly or through an XSLink Hub that bridges two USB-C streams into one fiber port. Ethernet is reserved for CrashCam systems via the Viper processor. Dual-connection cameras (Phoenix, Galileo) can use two channels for full frame rate or one channel at half speed. The Helios 8K requires GPU processing and is blocked from the RT III.
