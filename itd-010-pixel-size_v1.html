A pixel is a bucket for photons. A 2.5 µm pixel (Helios 8K) has an area of 6.25 µm². An 11.6 µm pixel (XS II) has an area of 134.6 µm², collecting over 21 times more light from the same scene. This is why the XS II achieves ISO 30,000 while the Helios sits at ISO 3,000. More photons per pixel means stronger signal relative to the sensor's read noise floor, which translates directly to cleaner images at the same exposure settings. The tradeoff is resolution: fitting fewer, larger pixels onto the same sensor area means fewer total pixels.
