Baseline and depth: Wider camera spacing (larger baseline) increases depth resolution because the triangulation angle between cameras grows. But too wide and the overlap region shrinks, reducing the usable measurement volume. Stereo PIV with two cameras recovers 2D velocity fields (two in-plane components). Adding a third and fourth camera at orthogonal angles enables tomographic or volumetric reconstruction of all three velocity components in 3D space.
