The physics: A CMOS sensor reads pixels row by row. Fewer rows means faster readout, which means higher frame rates. Reducing resolution (cropping the active sensor area) is the primary mechanism for increasing speed. Every mode in this chart represents a specific crop of the full sensor, chosen to balance spatial detail against temporal resolution for a given application.
