High-speed cameras record continuously into a circular memory buffer. Old frames are overwritten as new ones arrive. The trigger marks a moment in time and tells the camera where to stop overwriting. In end trigger (pre-trigger) mode, everything before the trigger is kept. In start trigger (post-trigger) mode, everything after is kept. Center trigger splits the buffer evenly around the event. The operator does not need to predict when the event will happen. The buffer already contains it.
