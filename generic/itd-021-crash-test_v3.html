The Camera Is A Ruler

A frontal crash lasts 120 milliseconds. The airbag fires 15 ms in. Peak head excursion happens near 90 ms. Pull the lever. Watch how many of those milliseconds your camera can actually resolve.

In crash certification the high-speed camera is not there to make the crash look cool. It is there as a measurement instrument. Engineers need to know when the airbag fired, how far the head traveled, whether peak excursion cleared the steering wheel. A 60 fps camera samples the 120 ms event seven times, one frame every 16.7 ms, so the airbag is already fully deployed by the time the second frame arrives. At 5,000 fps the same event is six hundred samples with 0.2 ms between them, and the head's trajectory resolves into a continuous curve. The crash didn't get slower. The ruler got finer.
