Rolling shutter reads pixel rows sequentially, top to bottom. Fast-moving subjects appear skewed because the top and bottom of the frame are captured at different moments. Global shutter exposes and reads all pixels simultaneously, eliminating motion artifacts but requiring more complex (and typically noisier) circuitry. BSI (back-side illuminated) sensors flip the silicon so light hits the photodiode directly, improving quantum efficiency by 40-90% over FSI (front-side illuminated) designs.
