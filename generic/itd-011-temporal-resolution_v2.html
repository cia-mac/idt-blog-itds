A ball crossing the frame at 10 m/s travels 333 mm between frames at 30 fps. At 1,000 fps that gap shrinks to 10 mm. At 15,000 fps it is 0.67 mm. The ball does not move differently. The camera simply records more of what actually happened. High-speed imaging does not slow down reality. It samples it more densely, revealing motion that was always there but invisible at lower frame rates.
