Exposure Time vs. Motion Blur

A longer shutter collects more light and more motion.

Exposure time controls how long the sensor collects photons for a single frame. A 1/30s exposure at 10 m/s produces 333 mm of motion blur. The subject smears across the frame. A 1/50,000s exposure (20 microseconds) freezes the same subject with only 0.2 mm of displacement, but collects 1,667 times less light. High-speed cameras compensate with larger pixels, higher ISO sensitivity, and brighter illumination. The exposure decision is always a tradeoff: short enough to freeze the motion, long enough to maintain signal quality.
