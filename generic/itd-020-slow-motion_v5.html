What Slow Motion Actually Is

A bullet passes through an apple in 5 milliseconds. At 60 fps you see a streak and a mess. Drag the lever to any frame rate between 60 and 20,000. The bullet slows, the apple ruptures in visible pieces, and you watch what was always there.

Frame Rates

Slow motion is not the camera bending time. The camera captures frames faster than it plays them back. Capture 20,000 per second, replay at 30, and a 5 millisecond bullet impact plays back over 3.3 seconds. The ratio is the slow-motion factor. Capture rate divided by playback rate. A phone at 60 fps plays the same impact back in 10 milliseconds, a streak. At 20,000 fps you get 666× slower, and you can see the bullet mid-apple. The bullet's speed is identical. The sampling is what changed.
