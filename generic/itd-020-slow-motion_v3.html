What Slow Motion Actually Is

A mousetrap snaps closed in 5 milliseconds. At 60 fps you see a blur. Drag the lever, raise the frame rate, and watch the bar swing across the base frame by frame. The mechanism is not slower. The camera sampled it denser.

Slow motion is not the camera bending time. The camera captures frames faster than it plays them back. Capture 20,000 per second, replay at 30 per second, and a 5 millisecond mousetrap snap plays back over 3.3 seconds. The ratio is the slow-motion factor. Capture rate divided by playback rate. A phone at 60 fps plays the same snap back in 10 milliseconds, still a blink. At 20,000 fps you get 666× slower. The spring force is identical. The sampling is what changed.
