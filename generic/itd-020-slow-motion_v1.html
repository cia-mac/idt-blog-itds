What Slow Motion Actually Is

A spark jumps the gap in 5 milliseconds. Your eye catches a flicker. Raise the frame rate and the same 5 ms stretches into a viewable event. The spark is not slower. The camera sampled it denser.

Slow motion is not the camera bending time. The camera captures frames faster than it plays them back. Capture 20,000 per second, replay at 30 per second, and a 5 millisecond spark plays back over 3 seconds. The ratio is the slow-motion factor. It is simply capture rate divided by playback rate. A phone at 60 fps plays back the same spark in 10 milliseconds, which is still a blink. At 20,000 fps you get 666× slower. The event is identical. The sampling is what changed.
