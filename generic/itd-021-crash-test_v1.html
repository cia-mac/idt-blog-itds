How Crash Tests Are Actually Measured

A car hits a barrier, the airbag deploys, the dummy pitches forward and rebounds. The whole event takes 50 milliseconds. At 60 fps you see a smear. Pull the lever up and watch every stage resolve frame by frame, which is how safety engineers measure what happens.

Frame Rates

Crash tests are not filmed for drama. They are filmed for measurement. The airbag must fully deploy in ~40 ms, before the dummy's head reaches its path. Engineers need to track the head's trajectory to within a millimeter to compute the Head Injury Criterion that certifies a vehicle. A 60 fps camera samples that trajectory every 16 ms, which is blind to most of the event. At 5,000 fps the airbag inflation resolves into discrete stages. At 20,000 fps a crash lab can measure dummy kinematics to fractions of a millimeter. Same 50 ms event. More samples, more truth.
