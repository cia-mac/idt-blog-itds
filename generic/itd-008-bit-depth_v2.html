A 1-bit image has 2 levels: black and white. An 8-bit image has 256 levels, enough for most display purposes but not for post-production. A 12-bit image has 4,096 levels, providing headroom to adjust exposure, recover highlights, and push shadows without introducing visible stepping artifacts. In high-speed imaging, 12-bit capture preserves detail in both the bright flash of an explosion and the dark interior of a vehicle cabin within the same frame. The difference is invisible in a properly exposed thumbnail but becomes obvious the moment you grade the footage.
